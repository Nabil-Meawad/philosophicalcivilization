# Foundational Notes

The Foundational Notes are a series of documents intended to define the starting points of inquiry within the Philosophical Civilization project.

They are not a philosophical system.

They are not a collection of final conclusions.

They are not protected assumptions.

Their purpose is limited to:

* identifying phenomena,
* formulating philosophical problems,
* defining central questions,
* proposing open hypotheses,
* and clarifying the methods governing subsequent inquiry.

The Foundational Notes do not seek to preserve their hypotheses.

They seek to preserve the conditions under which hypotheses may be criticized, revised, or abandoned.

The project does not protect its ideas from criticism.

It protects criticism from its ideas.

The project does not ask its hypotheses to survive.

It asks its method to deserve survival.
