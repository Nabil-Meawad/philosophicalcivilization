# Manifesto of Structural Civilizational Theory

## Premise
This work proposes a different way of understanding civilizations: not as historical narratives or isolated value systems, but as structured systems shaped by the continuous interaction between power, legitimacy, need, and stability, operating within a persistent gap between reality and its perception.

## Core Dynamics
A civilization is not defined solely by what it declares itself to be, but by how it reproduces its internal order over time and manages structural tensions under changing conditions.

## Justice
Within this framework, justice is not an absolute moral standard, but a functional element embedded in a broader system of organization—continuously reshaped without losing its internal logic.

## Perspective
This perspective does not offer moral judgment. It aims to analyze civilizational structures as they operate, rather than as they are conventionally described.

## Testable Claim
When the gap between perceived legitimacy and underlying need widens, structural reconfiguration tends to follow.
